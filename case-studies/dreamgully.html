DreamGully is our own studio — the first case study we shipped, because if we couldn't build our own brand, we shouldn't be building anyone else's. The work covered positioning, visual identity, website, and the content system that runs underneath it. The result is a studio brand that's clear about what it does, honest about how it does it, and built to be a working example of the kind of work we deliver to clients.

The Brief

Could we be a credible AI-native studio, or just another agency with an AI badge?

Most "AI agencies" are traditional agencies that added a ChatGPT subscription. The brief to ourselves was sharper: build a studio that's AI-native from the foundation up — where AI isn't a tool we use, it's how the studio operates. Every workflow, every deliverable, every client interaction should demonstrate the difference.

The brand had to work for two audiences at once: small service businesses in Melbourne and Dubai who are skeptical of agencies, and real estate brokerages who are drowning in marketing options. The studio brand had to be premium enough to charge mid-market rates, plain enough to feel approachable to a cafe owner, and sharp enough to attract the kind of clients we actually want to work with.

"If we can't build our own brand from zero, we have no business building anyone else's."
The Work

Eight deliverables. One studio. Thirty days.

[01 · Positioning & strategy doc]
Deliverable 01

Positioning & strategy

The foundation. Defined who DreamGully is for, what it does, and what it doesn't do. Wrote the positioning around "creative studio for the AI age" — sharp enough to differentiate, broad enough to grow into.

[02 · Brand identity system]
Deliverable 02

Brand identity system

Wordmark, color tokens, typography, motion principles, voice. The system holds the line: editorial but not precious, modern but not trendy. Cormorant Garamond for display, DM Sans for body, a blue gradient as the through-line.

[03 · Logo explorations]
Deliverable 03

Logo exploration

Eight rounds of wordmark and mark exploration before landing on the final lockup. The work is in the file: failed versions, reasoning, the moment it clicked. The log itself is a study in how the brand came to be.

[04 · Website — desktop + mobile]
Deliverable 04

Website

Single-page site built to do four things: communicate the offer, show the work, build trust, capture leads. Grey + blue gradient system. Editorial type. No stock photography. Fast, accessible, and built to convert.

[05 · Service offerings + packages]
Deliverable 05

Service packages

Three anchor packages — Brand Foundation, Growth Engine, Studio Retainer — each with a clear scope, a clear outcome, and a clear price. Custom quotes for everything else. The page explains what you get, what you don't, and what happens next.

[06 · Process & client deck]
Deliverable 06

Process & client deck

Four-step process — Discovery, Strategy, Creation, Launch — each step explained in plain language with what's delivered, when, and what the client does. The deck doubles as a sales tool for prospects and an onboarding doc for new clients.

[07 · Client intake system]
Deliverable 07

Client intake system

The form on the site routes inquiries into a structured intake. Every lead comes pre-qualified, with project type, budget range, timeline, and the question that tells us whether to take the call or pass. Built to filter, not just collect.

[08 · Content system]
Deliverable 08

Content system

The studio's own content is the working example of how we work for clients. Editorial still-lifes, prompt-engineered imagery, brand assets generated and curated. The system — not individual posts — is the deliverable. Replicable for any client engagement.
