FOAM & FOLD is the kind of local service that earns a permanent place in the weekly routine. The brand is mid-century-modern — warm cream, mustard, teal, an italic serif that feels optimistic without being twee. The kind of brand a discerning neighbour recommends by name: "Just go to Foam & Fold."

The Brief

If a suburban car wash wanted to feel like a third place.

The brief we wrote for this concept: build the brand a thoughtful local-services operator would commission if they wanted the car wash to feel like a destination, not a stop. The brand should be the kind of thing a passing pedestrian photographs and posts, not the kind of thing they ignore.

Constraint: nothing in the brand should signal "cheap." Mid-century-modern visual vocabulary — warm tones, italic serif, geometric shapes — applied with the discipline of a 2026 studio to a category that has historically under-designed itself.

"Just go to Foam & Fold. Trust me."
The Work

Two deliverables. A brand that earns its place in the routine.

[01 — WORDMARK, MASCOT, COLOUR, TYPE / 4:3]
DELIVERABLE 01

Brand identity system

Wordmark in an italic geometric serif (think Fraunces Italic). Palette: warm cream, mustard, teal, a single coral for the laundry-basket mascot. Type: italic display for headlines, sans for body. Mascot: a friendly laundry basket with eyes — designed, not childish, the kind of mark that ages well. Photography: sunny, suburban, real cars and real towels.

[02 — STOREFRONT, BAG, LOYALTY CARD, SOCIAL / 4:3]
DELIVERABLE 02

Supporting collateral

Storefront sign in cream and mustard, paper bag with the wordmark in teal, a loyalty card stamped with a custom brass die, branded receipt with the mascot peeking. Social templates in the same palette. The collateral is the brand in the customer's hand — it should feel like a small gift every time.

Outcome

A designed system, not a shipped business.

CONCEPT STUDY. No real business outcomes. The identity and collateral shown are designed to demonstrate how a mid-century-modern local services brand could be built: wordmark, mascot, type system, and a set of touchpoints that work in the customer's hand and on a Saturday morning. Use this as a reference for the kind of brand we build, not a claim about a real client.
